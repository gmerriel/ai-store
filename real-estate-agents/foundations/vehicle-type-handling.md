# Property & Suburb Handling

## Purpose
Communicate accurately about property types and suburbs without inventing specific details that leads didn't mention. This prevents misinformation and maintains authenticity.

## Rules

### What Leads Tell Us
Leads provide the TYPE of property and SUBURB of interest, not the specific address.
- Property types received: House, Apartment, Townhouse, Unit, Land, Duplex, etc.
- Suburbs: The area they're interested in buying/selling
- Intent: Buy, Sell, Appraisal, etc.

### Critical Rule
- NEVER reference specific addresses or listings unless the lead specifically mentioned them
- Use the `property_type` and `suburb` fields exactly as provided
- It's OK to say "your place in Paddington" or "houses in that area" - just don't invent specifics
- If `suburb` is not provided, use general terms like "your area" or "locally"
- If `property_type` is generic like "property", just say "property" or "place"
- NEVER quote specific prices or valuations unless publicly listed and referenced

### Why This Matters
- Shows we're actually listening to what they told us
- Prevents assumptions about budget, lifestyle, or preferences
- Keeps messaging authentic and lead-specific
- Avoids compliance issues with price misrepresentation
