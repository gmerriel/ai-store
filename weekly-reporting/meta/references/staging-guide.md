# Video Staging Guide Reference

Detailed staging directions for video ad hooks. Used by Skill 4 when generating Location + Prop + Action for each hook variant. All locations and props listed below are pre-approved for trades business owner content. Client-specific guides should be added per account.

---

## How Staging Works

Only the **hook** section of a video script gets staging directions. The body delivers straight to camera — no staging needed after the hook opens.

The hook staging must:
1. Instantly place the viewer in a recognisable situation for the niche
2. Include a prop that signals "this person is in the trades"
3. Have an action that does NOT block speech (see Blocked Actions)
4. Create an authentic feel — not studio, not polished

## Approved Locations

### White Ute/HiLux/Ranger Cab
**When to use:** Morning routine hooks, reflection hooks, "I realised something" hooks
**Setup:** Driver's seat, engine off, parked. Morning light through windscreen. Dashboard visible.
**Notes:** This is the most universally recognised tradie location. Works for almost any hook. Shot from passenger-side angle or phone mounted on dash. No seatbelt needed — engine off.

### Residential Job Site (Active Build)
**When to use:** On-the-tools hooks, "just finished a job" hooks, discovery hooks mid-day
**Setup:** Timber frame, brick veneer, or concrete slab visible in background. Outdoor light. Dust or sawdust normal.
**Notes:** Background activity (other workers, machinery parked) adds authenticity. Do not have loud machinery operating during speech. No hard hat worn — held or clipped to vest.

### Site Shed / Back Office (Job Site Admin)
**When to use:** Admin/numbers hooks, pricing reveal hooks, "paperwork at end of day" hooks
**Setup:** Folding table, paperwork spread, clipboards. Functional — not clean. Industrial fluorescent lighting acceptable.
**Notes:** This location works for hooks about the business side of trades. Having an invoice or quote sheet visible on the desk adds credibility.

### Workshop / Garage (Trade Environment)
**When to use:** Team/process hooks, "I figured out a system" hooks, tool reveal hooks
**Setup:** Tools on pegboard in background. Concrete floor. Work bench. Natural or industrial lighting.
**Notes:** Do not have power tools running. Background should look organised-but-used, not showroom.

### Ute Tailgate / Tray (End of Day)
**When to use:** "I figured something out today" hooks, tired-but-realised hooks, end-of-day reflection
**Setup:** Tailgate down, tools or equipment visible in tray behind. Late afternoon light.
**Notes:** Leaning against the tailgate works well. Props should be trade tools (not personal items). This location signals "end of long day".

### Kitchen Table (Night — Paperwork)
**When to use:** Sacrifice/family hooks, late-night admin hooks, "doing the books at 10pm" hooks
**Setup:** Kitchen or dining table with paperwork spread. Warm overhead light. Dark outside window visible if possible.
**Notes:** This location carries the highest emotional weight — it signals work has followed the performer home. Use when hook is about personal sacrifice or discovering a better way after struggling.

## Approved Props

### Clipboard with Handwritten Quote Sheet
**Best use:** Any hook involving pricing, quoting, estimating jobs
**Signal:** "This person quotes jobs" — immediately identifies business owner vs employee
**Rules:** Writing must be handwritten (not printed). Partial view is fine. No company logo visible.

### Hard Hat — HELD, Not Worn
**Best use:** On-site hooks, "just came off a job" hooks
**Signal:** Fast niche identification — hard hat = construction/trades
**Rules:** MUST be held (in hand, on tailgate, clipped to belt) — not worn. Worn hard hat blocks natural head movement during speech and makes delivery stilted.

### High-Vis Vest Over Work Shirt
**Best use:** Any location — fastest possible niche signal
**Signal:** This is the single most efficient prop for trades identification
**Rules:** Worn over a work shirt (not corporate shirt). No company branding visible. Unzipped is fine.

### Set of Trade Keys
**Best use:** Ute cab, end-of-day hooks
**Signal:** Ute keys + site access fob = business owner who drives to jobs
**Rules:** Hold casually or place on dashboard/seat. Do not jingle or fiddle during speech.

### Takeaway Coffee Cup
**Best use:** Morning hooks, ute cab
**Signal:** Trades morning routine — coffee before the day starts
**Rules:** **NOT being drunk from during hook.** Hold in hand or place on dashboard. Drinking = mouth occupied = cannot speak. Can be referenced in hook text ("my $6 coffee while I figured this out").

### Printed Invoice or Quote Document
**Best use:** Office/shed hooks, pricing/numbers hooks
**Signal:** Real paperwork = real business
**Rules:** Numbers visible on the document should be believable (real invoice format, not round numbers).

### Work Gloves
**Best use:** Site hooks, tailgate hooks
**Signal:** This person works with their hands
**Rules:** Tucked into belt or held in hand — not worn on hands during speech. On-hands = can't gesture naturally.

### Phone/Tablet Showing Relevant Screen
**Best use:** Tool reveal hooks, calculator hooks, app demo hooks
**Signal:** This person uses digital tools in their trade
**Rules:** Screen must be visible and legible. Must show relevant content (calculator, spreadsheet, invoice app). Not a social media feed.

## Blocked Actions

These actions are hard-blocked. If Skill 4 generates a staging action containing any of these, the script is flagged `action_valid = False` and must be revised.

| Blocked Action | Reason |
|----------------|--------|
| Drinking or eating anything | Mouth occupied — physically cannot deliver clean speech |
| Operating power tools (drill, saw, grinder) | Noise + safety hazard — cannot film while operating |
| Driving | Safety + camera angle limitation — moving vehicle can't hold stable frame |
| On a phone call | Audience is confused — who is the performer talking to? |
| Hammering, sawing, drilling (even nearby) | Ambient noise will drown speech |
| Carrying a heavy load | Cannot speak comfortably — restricted breath and posture |
| Wearing a hard hat (speech delivery) | Restricts head movement, changes energy, looks stilted |

## Combining Location + Prop + Action

### Example Combinations

**Hook type: Morning realisation ("I worked 237 days straight")**
```
Location : White ute cab, driver's seat, engine off, morning light
Prop     : Takeaway coffee cup on dashboard
Action   : Looking ahead, turns to camera
Energy   : Calm but resolved — like they've just processed something
```

**Hook type: Admin/numbers reveal ("My apprentice asked how much I made per hour")**
```
Location : Site shed / back office, paperwork on desk
Prop     : Clipboard with handwritten quote sheet
Action   : Looking at clipboard, looks up to camera with slight pause
Energy   : Surprised/realising — genuine moment of recognition
```

**Hook type: On-tools discovery ("I just finished a $4,000 job and made $300")**
```
Location : Residential job site — timber frame visible in background
Prop     : High-vis vest, hard hat HELD in left hand
Action   : Standing, looks at camera directly
Energy   : Controlled frustration — not angry, more exhausted-honest
```

**Hook type: System reveal ("Here's what I wrote on the whiteboard")**
```
Location : Workshop / garage — tools on pegboard in background
Prop     : Marker in hand (just finished writing)
Action   : Steps back from whiteboard, turns to camera
Energy   : Confident — "let me show you what I figured out"
```

## Client-Specific Staging Notes

### Profitable Tradie
- Primary location: White ute cab or job site
- Primary prop: Hard hat (held) + high-vis vest
- Energy range: Honest/direct → Surprised/realising → Confident/authoritative
- Avoid: Any corporate office setting — this audience is on tools, not at a desk
